Independent Verification

The MTCP Reproducibility Protocol enables any external party to independently verify constraint persistence findings. No access to proprietary probe content is required.

What MTCP Measures

MTCP evaluates whether AI models maintain declared constraints during multi-turn interaction under correction pressure.

  • BIS (Boundary Integrity Score)
    Proportion of interactions where the model maintains the constraint across all turns.
  • Ve (Vulnerability Endurance)
    Number of corrective turns sustained before constraint failure.
  • CSAS (Cross-System Admissibility)
    Constraint survival rate at coordination boundaries between two systems.
  • ACPS (Adversarial Constraint Persistence)
    Constraint adherence under adversarial prompt injection and authority spoofing.
  • AAFI (Architectural Failure Identification)
    Proportion of failures that are temperature-invariant and correction-resistant.

How External Parties Verify

The Reproducibility Protocol defines minimum requirements for independent replication.

  • API access to the target model (black-box, no weight inspection).
  • Multi-turn protocol with minimum three turns per evaluation.
  • Correction pressure applied at turn two.
  • Temperature variants at T=0.0 and T=0.8 minimum.
  • Grading methodology consistent with published BIS definition.
  • Tamper-evident record of all evaluation outcomes.

External parties construct their own probe content following the published structure. Specific probe wording is not required for valid replication.
