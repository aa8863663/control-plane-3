Control Probe Degradation

Results from the 20-probe concealed control set. Control Probe Degradation (CPD) measures the performance gap between primary and control probes.

Control probes use identical constraint types to the primary evaluation but with novel topics and phrasing not present in the public dataset. A model scoring 90% on primary probes and 25% on control probes is not contradictory — the degradation is the expected pattern and the key diagnostic metric. Large negative CPD values indicate potential prior exposure to MTCP methodology during training. Do not compare control BIS directly to primary BIS as an absolute performance measure.
