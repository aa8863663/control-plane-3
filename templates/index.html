{# ── PROBLEM EXPLANATION ───────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

Why MTCP Exists

Most benchmarks test whether a model follows an instruction once. MTCP tests whether the model maintains a corrected constraint across interaction.

Constraint Persistence

Models must maintain corrected constraints across conversation turns, not only follow initial instructions.

Multi-turn correction sequences

Control Probe Integrity

Concealed control probes detect whether models have been exposed to evaluation methodology during training.

Control Probe Degradation (CPD)

Temperature Stability

Behaviour must remain consistent across temperature variation, not only perform at T=0.0.

Temporal Stability Index (TSI)

{# ── WHAT MTCP DETECTS ─────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

What MTCP Detects

Constraint Persistence Failure

Models revert to uncorrected behaviour after correction. MTCP detects this through multi-turn interaction probes.

Measured by Boundary Integrity Score

Control Probe Degradation

Performance gaps between primary and concealed control probes indicate potential methodology exposure during training.

Measured by Control Probe Degradation

Temperature Instability

Models show inconsistent behaviour across temperature settings. MTCP measures stability across T=0.0, 0.2, 0.5, and 0.8.

Measured by Temporal Stability Index
