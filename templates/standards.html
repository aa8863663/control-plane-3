Standards Contribution

MTCP proposes constraint persistence measurement as a foundational element for AI governance standards.

Empirical Foundation for AI Governance

AI governance frameworks specify requirements for system behaviour. They do not define empirical protocols for verifying constraint persistence across multi-turn interactions. Single-turn benchmarks miss constraint drift, temperature-invariant architectural failures, and cross-system coordination failures. MTCP addresses this measurement gap with independently reproducible metrics and grading criteria.

Four measurement constructs are proposed as standards contributions. BIS as a per-decision constraint persistence metric. AAFI as an architectural failure classification metric. Correction Ceiling as a deployment readiness threshold. Temperature Invariance Classification as an architectural versus stochastic failure distinction.

Target Standards Bodies

MTCP constraint persistence measurement is being submitted to three standards bodies.

  • BSI AI Standards Committee
    UK national standards body. AI safety and governance measurement standards.
  • EU AI Office
    European Union AI governance body. Empirical measurement infrastructure for EU AI Act compliance verification.
  • CENELEC JTC 21
    European standards body for AI. Via Matteo Prandi at Icaro Lab. Review of MTCP metric definitions against emerging measurement standards.

The standards submission document is available on request. Contact admin@mtcp.live.
