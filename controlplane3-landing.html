Research Findings · March 2026

What MTCP Reveals

Finding 01
Open source outperforms paid Claude on MTCP
LLaMA 3.3 70B averages 75.6% — a full 7.5 points above Claude Sonnet 4 at 68.1%, despite Sonnet costing significantly more per token. Size and price do not predict MTCP compliance.
Finding 02
Temperature has near-zero effect on Claude
Claude Haiku spans only 2% across all four temperatures. Sonnet scored identically at T=0.5 and T=0.8. This indicates systematic post-correction failures, not sampling randomness.
Finding 03
Higher temperature degrades LLaMA compliance
LLaMA 3.1 8B drops 7 points from T=0.2 to T=0.8. LLaMA 3.3 70B stays flat across all temperatures. This divergence suggests different failure modes between model families.
Finding 04
No model has passed MTCP yet
The best result — LLaMA 3.3 70B at T=0.0 with 76.6% — is still a grade C. A passing grade requires 80%+. MTCP is a genuinely hard safety benchmark for production use.
Methodology

The MTCP Protocol

Multi-Turn Constraint Persistence tests whether models maintain compliance after explicit correction — the most relevant safety metric for real-world deployments where human oversight occurs.

💬
Turn 1 — Initial Probe
Model receives a constrained prompt. Compliance is assessed against the constraint vector (NCA, SFC, IDL, CG, or LANG).
⚠️
Turn 2 — Correction
If T1 violated, model receives an explicit correction. Recovery is measured. Persistent violation triggers T3.
🛑
Turn 3 — Hard Stop
If T2 violated, a reinforced correction is issued. Continued violation triggers SAFETY_HARD_STOP — logged, terminated, counted.
